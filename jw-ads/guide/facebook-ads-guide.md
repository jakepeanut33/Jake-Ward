# How to Set Up Facebook Ads for Clients — JW Ads Playbook

## Step 1: Set Up Your Own Business Manager First

Before you touch a client's account, set up yours at business.facebook.com.

1. Go to business.facebook.com → Create Account
2. Name it "JW Ads"
3. This is your agency hub — all client accounts connect here

---

## Step 2: Onboard a New Client

### What you need from every client:
- Access to their Facebook Business Page (ask them to add you as an admin)
- Their ad budget (you recommend minimum $300-500/month ad spend)
- Their goal: leads, calls, website traffic, or foot traffic

### How to get access:
1. In your Business Manager → go to **Business Settings → Accounts → Ad Accounts**
2. Click **Add → Request Access to an Ad Account**
3. Enter their Ad Account ID (they find this in their Ads Manager)
4. OR: create a new Ad Account for them inside your Business Manager

---

## Step 3: Install the Facebook Pixel (IMPORTANT)

The Pixel tracks what people do after clicking your ad. Always install it.

1. Business Manager → **Events Manager → Connect Data Sources → Web**
2. Name it "[Client Name] Pixel"
3. Give them the pixel code to add to their website (or use the Partner Integration if they use Squarespace/Shopify/Wix — it's one click)
4. Verify it's firing at **pixelhelper.facebook.com**

---

## Step 4: Campaign Structure

Every campaign has 3 levels:

```
Campaign (the goal)
  └── Ad Set (who sees it + budget)
        └── Ad (what they see)
```

### For local businesses, use this setup:

**Campaign:**
- Objective: **Leads** (if collecting phone/email) or **Traffic** (if sending to website)

**Ad Set:**
- Location: Kansas City, MO — 10-15 mile radius
- Age: 25-55 (adjust per niche)
- Budget: $10-20/day to start
- Placements: Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed only (uncheck everything else)

**Ad:**
- Format: Single image or short video (15s max)
- Headline: Direct benefit ("Get 20% Off Your First Visit")
- Primary text: Short — 2-3 sentences max
- CTA button: "Call Now" or "Get Quote" or "Learn More"

---

## Step 5: The Winning Ad Formula for Local Businesses

**Hook (first line):** Call out their location or problem
> "Kansas City homeowners — is your lawn embarrassing your house?"

**Body:** What you offer + social proof
> "We handle everything from mowing to full landscaping. 200+ happy customers in KC."

**CTA:** Make it dead simple
> "Call us today for a free quote → (816) 555-0123"

**Image:** Real photos always beat stock. Ask the client for photos of their work/shop/team.

---

## Step 6: Lead Form Ads (Best for Most Local Businesses)

Instead of sending people to a website, use Facebook's built-in Lead Form.
People fill it out without leaving Facebook = way more leads.

1. At the Ad level → CTA: "Get Quote" → Instant Form
2. Create a form: Name, Phone Number, "What service are you interested in?"
3. Connect to their email via **Zapier** (free tier works) so leads go straight to their inbox
4. Charge extra for this setup — it's high value

## Step 8: The Cold Call Script

When you call a business owner:

> "Hey, is this [Name]? Hey, my name's Jake — I run a small ad agency here in KC called JW Ads.
> I'm not trying to sell you anything crazy, but I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have any ads
> running on Facebook right now and I had a quick idea I thought could bring you more customers.
> Do you have like 2 minutes?"

If yes:
> "So basically what I do is run Facebook and Instagram ads for local businesses like yours.
> I set everything up, manage it, and you just get the leads. I'm working with a few [niche]
> businesses in KC right now and they're seeing [X] new customers a month from it.
> Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week to see if it could work for you?"

**Handle objections:**
- *"I tried Facebook ads before and it didn't work"* → "That's actually really common — most people set them up wrong. What were you doing when you ran them?" (Get them talking, then offer to fix it)
- *"How much does it cost?"* → "Ad spend starts around $300-500/month, and my management fee is $500. But let's talk first and make sure it's even a fit."
- *"Send me some info"* → "I'd rather just hop on a quick call — what's better for you, mornings or afternoons this week?"

---

## Step 9: Reporting to Clients

Every month send them a simple message:

> "Hey [Name]! Here's your ad update for [Month]:
> - Ads reached: X people
> - Clicks: X
> - Leads: X
> - Cost per lead: $X
>
> [1 line on what you're changing next month]
> Let me know if you have any questions!"

Keep it short. Clients don't want a 10-page report, they want to know it's working.

## Niches That Work Best for Facebook Ads

1. **Barbershops / Salons** — easy to target, visual, high repeat customers
2. **Dentists** — high ticket, "new patient" offers work great
3. **Gyms / Personal Trainers** — January and summer are goldmines
4. **Restaurants** — promotions, events, happy hour
5. **Contractors / Home Services** — roofing, HVAC, landscaping
6. **Chiropractors / Massage** — offer a discounted first visit
