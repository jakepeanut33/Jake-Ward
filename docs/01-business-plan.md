# 1 · Business Plan

## The model in one paragraph

You run an online store (Snugpaw) selling cozy and personalized pet products. You
don't hold inventory. When a customer buys, you place the same order with a supplier
who ships it directly to the customer ("dropshipping"). Your profit is the gap
between your retail price and the supplier cost + ad cost + fees. Your real job is
**marketing and customer trust**, not warehousing.

## Why pets, why personalized

- **Emotional buyers.** People spend on pets like family — they don't price-shop a
  gift for their dog the way they price-shop a phone charger.
- **Personalization kills price comparison.** A custom photo blanket can't be found
  cheaper on Amazon, so you keep healthy margins (40–60%).
- **Repeat customers.** Pet owners come back for the next bed, toy, or gift.
- **Strong 2026 demand.** Personalized pet accessories showed the strongest recent
  demand signal, and calming beds/soft blankets are proven sellers in the $18–$40
  "sweet spot" where ads still leave room for profit. (Sources in file 2.)

## The brand: Snugpaw

- **Name:** Snugpaw (warm, cozy, brandable, easy to say/spell).
- **Promise:** "Cozy, personalized comfort for the pets you love."
- **Look:** warm cream + terracotta + sage palette (already in the storefront).
- **Voice:** friendly, heartfelt, a little playful. We sell *feelings* (love, comfort,
  guilt-relief, gifting) more than specs.
- **Hero products:** Custom Pet Portrait Blanket + Calming Donut Bed.

> Before launch, check the name is free: a quick search on Shopify's domain checker,
> the US trademark database (uspto.gov TESS), and Instagram/TikTok handle
> availability. If "Snugpaw" is taken in your market, alternates: Pawnest, Snugtail,
> Cozypaw, Pawloom.

## What success looks like (realistic)

| Phase | Timeframe | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Validate** | Weeks 1–3 | Store live, pixel firing, first ad tests run. Goal: learn which product/audience gets cheap clicks + any sales. Likely break-even or small loss. |
| **First profit** | Weeks 4–8 | Find one product + audience that's profitable at small scale ($10–30/day). |
| **Scale** | Month 3+ | Slowly increase budget on winners, add upsells, improve creatives. |

Most beginners do **not** profit in week one. The winners are the ones who survive
the learning phase with their budget intact. Budget your ad money so a bad first
test doesn't end the business.

## Legal / responsibility notes (read these)

- **You are running a real business.** Depending on where you live, you may need to
  register a business, collect sales tax, and report income. If you're a minor, a
  parent/guardian typically must own the Shopify and payment accounts and any
  business registration. Talk to a parent before taking payments.
- **Ad platform rules.** Meta requires advertisers to follow its policies and (in
  many places) be 18+. Don't make health claims you can't back up ("cures anxiety" →
  say "helps pets feel calm and secure").
- **Truthful marketing.** Realistic shipping times, honest "compare-at" prices, and
  genuine reviews only. Fake scarcity and fake reviews violate platform rules and
  consumer-protection law.
- This playbook's policy/legal pages are **starter templates** — have an adult review
  them for your location before launch.
