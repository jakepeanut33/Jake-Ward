# 6 · Financials & Unit Economics

The whole game is one question: **does each sale make more than it costs to get?**

## Unit economics of one sale (Custom Pet Portrait Blanket)

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Retail price | $39.95 |
| − Product cost (supplier) | −$12.50 |
| − Shipping (you cover, or build into price) | −$4.00 |
| − Payment processing (~3% + $0.30) | −$1.50 |
| **= Margin before ads** | **≈ $21.95** |
| − Ad cost per sale (CPA, varies) | −$? |
| **= Net profit per sale** | **margin − CPA** |

So if it costs you **$15 in ads** to get a sale, you net ~**$7**. If it costs **$25**,
you **lose ~$3**. This is why creative and conversion rate matter so much.

## Break-even ROAS (memorize this)

**Break-even ROAS = Price ÷ Margin-before-ads.**

For the blanket: 39.95 ÷ 21.95 ≈ **1.82**. Meaning:
- ROAS **below ~1.8** → losing money.
- ROAS **~1.8** → break even.
- ROAS **above ~2** → profit. Above ~2.5 → scale hard.

Calculate this for every product and write it on a sticky note. It's your go/no-go line.

## How to push profit up (these compound)

1. **Raise Average Order Value (AOV).** The cheapest profit there is.
   - **Bundles:** "Bed + Blanket gift set." "Buy 2 tags, save 15%."
   - **Post-purchase upsell apps** (e.g. one-click upsell) to add the ID tag or hair
     remover after checkout.
   - **Free-shipping threshold at $45** nudges people to add a second item.
2. **Improve conversion rate.** Better photos, reviews, faster page, clearer guarantee.
   Going from 1% → 2% literally halves your effective CPA.
3. **Email/SMS** (Klaviyo free tier): abandoned-cart + welcome flows recover sales you
   already paid to acquire. Often 10–25% of revenue, near-free.
4. **Retargeting ads** to cart-abandoners — cheapest sales you'll get.

## Simple monthly projection (recommended $300–500 plan)

Illustrative, **not a promise** — early months often lose money while you learn.

| | Pessimistic | Realistic (after finding a winner) | Good |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ad spend | $400 | $400 | $400 |
| ROAS | 1.3 | 2.0 | 2.8 |
| Revenue | $520 | $800 | $1,120 |
| Product+ship+fees (~45% of rev) | −$234 | −$360 | −$504 |
| Ad spend | −$400 | −$400 | −$400 |
| Shopify+apps | −$60 | −$60 | −$60 |
| **Net profit** | **−$174** | **−$20** | **+$156** |

Takeaways:
- It's completely normal to be **negative or break-even for the first 1–2 months.**
  You're paying to find a winning product+creative.
- Once a winner hits ROAS ~2.5+, scaling spend scales profit — that's the flywheel.
- If after ~$300–400 of honest testing nothing clears break-even, **change the product
  or the creative** — don't just add more budget.

## Track these weekly (a simple spreadsheet is fine)

- Ad spend, revenue, **ROAS**, orders, **CPA**, AOV, conversion rate, profit.
- Per product: which is carrying the store? Cut dead weight, double the winner.

## Money safety rules

- **Only spend ad money you can afford to lose** while learning.
- Keep a buffer for refunds/chargebacks — always fulfill and support fast to avoid them.
- Separate the business money from personal money (and if you're a minor, an adult owns
  the accounts and handles taxes).
